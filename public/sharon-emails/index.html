With regards to your topic list;

Head Coverings: With regards to this it is always a difficult topic. Clearly the intention around head coverings is about submission to relevant others, from God down to man then women. I would say this isn’t just related to marriage (sure that you didn’t only mean that when you said the husband is the head of the wife as you later refer also to male role in church ministry but just in case) but more of a role of men and women thing more widely. As a single women i should still submit to my male counterparts in their authority and also me being the glory of man is not about being married either. I to agree that it is right to reject the cultural relativity argument and the presence of the angels in v 10 of 1 Cor 11 really hammers that home for me even more. This would also say to me that as angels still exist and that hasn’t changed neither has the covering on head teachings being an outward sign of my inward submission and a right thing to do. Paul clearly says there is no other practice in the churches of God so also doesn’t limit the practice to Corinth or that time particularly that i can see. However, it is very difficult to say that someone’s intention and heart is not important. We know God looks at the heart and i’m sure a women who wears a hat but has many things wrong in her heart might be worse than the other way around. However, I would wonder why a women who agrees that the intention of wearing head coverings is to signify submission would not wear a hat as its not a painful thing to have to do; its a very simple outward sign for the inward submission in a church context. Furthermore, if i compare it to other areas e.g. baptism, that is also an outward symbol of intention to follow Christ and i would see that as as essential step of a disciple and would likewise urge a believer who had the intent to follow him to demonstrate it with the step Christ commanded rather than just hold the intent without the action that follows this and shows it outwardly to others. I don’t think either of them would signify someone wasn’t saved or be part of a bigger issues in that sense, only God will be able to judge at the end for some things what was the right thing and this may be one of those areas. However, i would question why any female wouldn’t do this if they recognize it is an appropriate sign of submission.

  • I specifically used the husband/wife phrase as I don’t see clear evidence that all men are the head of all women. Ephesians 5 makes it clear that a husband is head of the wife, but historically, there was no head over a divorced or widowed women (I would extend to any women not under the roof of their father’s house too). ESV renders it “head of a wife is her husband”. Later it say’s women reflects man’s glory. Do all women reflect all men’s glory? Or Just the wife of the husband. Do you reflect my glory? I think this is a directs relation to the Eph 5 teaching
  • Why do you believe this is refering to all men being head of all women? Isn’t this the same teaching as ephesians 5? I watched a great teaching on this and how it tied in with rebecca and isaac in Gen 24 when she put on her head covering when she saw him in the distance as a sign of submission to her husband. Also went into the teaching in numbers on vows. If a married women made a vow, the husband could nullify or intercede the vow. Same goes with young woman is under her father’s house. However if you a are a widow or divorcee then you have no such head-covering and are under oth to fulfil the vow as man was. I believe these contexts can be applied when interpreting 1 Cor 11.

Shake of faith: What you said about this was really interesting and struck me quite a lot, in fact i was very thankful for God using you to speak about this. There have been times over the last few years where I’ve definitely struggled more with my faith than i have in my earlier years. I had put it down to a variety of issues including life circumstances. me not putting as much time into personal study and prayer etc. However, it gave me a jolt when you talked about the struggles people have who belong to a “one true” church/religion and then have issues with that . I wondered whether that had contributed more to some of my issues over the last few years than i would have realised without you saying this. I think its definitely been difficult in the church over the last few years too, especially when comparing the church to others e.g. seeing how small the church is, that it isn’t growing/developing people like i would hope, seeing issues with the way some things are run (lack of study, difficulties placing the roles of other churches etc), seeming lack of first love and enthusiasm amongst the church etc. If i’m honest there have been times over the last few years where I have questioned all that i have learnt, not just about churches of God but more widely to even the existence of God itself. I haven’t walked away or felt unable to continue my faith but it’s been tough at times and i certainly drifted farther and had more apathy towards God and his things than i feel i would like to admit. I think this is something any christian can go through, having read other interesting articles about this by an individual outside of Churches of God. However, I very much identified with some of the issues you raised around feeling you couldn’t advocate for people to come to the church and how sad it is to be in that position. As i said i had even visited other churches with the intention of exploring what was out there and to actually get some teaching locally. At the moment i’m still in a place where i feel i have responsibilities and commitments to the local churches and don’t feel that God wants me to be somewhere else. I have a burden to try and help things change/be different and recognise a large part of that is for me to concentrate on being more devoted to my own study and prayer times, meeting with other christians for study etc. I’m not saying i think the Churches of God are the only ones out there which God is pleased with and i never have felt that, however, i do feel its the place God wants me to be right now.
