Paul gives guidance on conduct of women when praying and prophesying in church. This post attempts to deal with the percieved contradiction between this passage and 1 Cor 14.

But I want you to know that Christ is the head of every man(Gk. aner), and the man(Gk. aner) is the head of a woman(Gk. gyne), and God is the head of Christ. — 1 Corinthians 11:3

Is the correct translation in v3 “man” or “husband”?

The Greek words for man and woman in the NET have some ambiguity. Most translations (NIV, KJV, NASB) render these words the same as the NET with man and woman. However, a few translations differ.

  • AMP: the head of a woman is her husband
  • YLT: the head of a woman is the husband
  • ESV: the head of a wife is her husband

^ the ESV goes one step further by rendering the word for woman as “wife” is to match the context of “husband”

”Paul wants the Corinthians to understand that Christ is the head of every man (more likely ‘husband’), and the ‘husband’ is head of every woman (more likely ‘wife’, given the ambiguity of these words in Greek).” — The New Bible Commentary

^ This view is further confirmed in the New Bible Commentary notes on 1 Cor 11.

^ So why the ambiguity? I believe this is answered by first looking at what Paul means when he talks about “headship”.

What does Paul mean by “headship”?

^ Paul is talking to a church mainly populated by Jewish people so it would be first of all be useful to explore what the Jewish understood about the term “headship”.

Rebekah looked up and saw Isaac. She got down from her camel and asked Abraham’s servant, “Who is that man walking in the field toward us?” “That is my master,” the servant replied. So she took her veil and covered herself. — Genesis 24:64–65

^ Through this act, Rebekah demonstrated that she was ready and willing to accept the the headship of Isaac through the bond of marriage. Dating back to Genesis, head coverings were a cultural sign of submission to a husband.

Are all brothers in Christ the head of all sisters in Christ?

^ “But wait, hold on…1 Corinthians 11 is in the context of the church while Ephesian 5 is in the Context of a marriage. Doesn’t 1 Cor11 extend this headship to all men in the church, not just the husband?”

^ Short answer: No.

…WORK IN PROGRESS…

because the husband(aner) is the head of the wife(gyne) as also Christ is the head of the church—he himself being the savior of the body. — Ephesians 5:23

^ Going back to Ephesians 5, Paul brings up a clear link to headship between the whole church and the intercession that took place on the cross

Let us rejoice and exult and give him glory, because the wedding celebration of the Lamb has come, and his bride has made herself ready. — Revelation 19:7

Because of this, the context must be considered. The ESV chose to render gyne as “wife” in both instances (both Ephesians and 1Corinthinians). I asked an elder about this ambiguity “who is the head of a widow in both church and at home?” The answer I got was that when they are at home, Christ is their head and when they are gathered together as a church all men are their head.

Revelations gives the clear picture that Jesus bears the church’s iniquities through marriage of the church as a whole.

But I do not allow a woman to teach or exercise authority over a man. She must remain quiet. — 1 Timothy 2:12

This is indicated in Num 30 where if a woman is under the care of her father or husband, the father/husband can “overrule” or “bear the iniquity”. However, if you are a widow or divorced, you must fulfil that vow. This suggests to me that if you are unmarried and not living in your father’s house then Christ is your head and not “all men”.
