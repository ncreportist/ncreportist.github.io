“if we are in that which claims to be God’s House; and yet the Christian cannot behave himself as these words require, nay, where these words are neither obeyed nor could be, because of the orginisation and its dicta, is it not clear that such is not House of God at all?”

So here the writer is suggesting that any church who are unable to persuade its members to be obedient to their teaching, cannot be considered a member the house of God

I’d like to challenge this by looking at 2 scenarios. In both scenaries, “Molly” has just joined a new church. In this new church, the teaching is very clear that women must wear a headcovering when praying in church. Molly has grown up to be falsely taught that heading coverings were a cultural issue specifically for the Corinth church and that wedding rings are now the cultural sign of submission. Consider these 2 ways to deal with this:

  1. Molly is approached by an elder after the meeting and is instructed that she must wear a head covering when praying in church. Molly is not persuaded that that is the truth but submits regardless.
  2. Molly is not instructed to wear a head covering but when the topic comes up, the teaching is still pro-head covering. Molly is not persuaded by the teaching and continues not to wear a headcovering when praying in church.The church leaders hope Molly will eventually be persuaded but do not enforce the doctrine upon her.

In both scenarios, Molly is still not persuaded that head coverings are a biblical doctrine. This is not a case of disobedience to God, but a case of ignorance.
